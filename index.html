Methodology & Variable Explanations

This model evaluates the mathematical viability of a restaurant concept independent of its specific capital structure. Whether fully self-funded, backed by external equity partners, or utilizing commercial debt, the underlying unit economics determine the success of the operation. Below is an overview of the core variables and calculations used.

Total Capital Required & Setup Costs

Calculates the absolute Day-0 cash required to open the doors. This incorporates structural improvements (Buildout), equipment and furniture (FF&E), organizational expenses incurred prior to revenue generation (Pre-Opening Costs like legal, permits, and initial training), and a Working Capital buffer to sustain operations before profitability is achieved.

Annual Break-Even Sales

The exact top-line revenue required for the restaurant to yield a net income of $0. This is calculated using the Contribution Margin ratio: Fixed Costs (Rent, Salaries, Utilities, G&A, Interest) divided by the Gross Margin percentage after Variable Costs (COGS, Hourly Labor, Marketing, Maintenance, Credit Card Fees).

EBITDA vs. Free Cash Flow (FCF)

EBITDA measures operational profitability before accounting nuances. Free Cash Flow represents true liquidity. The model calculates FCF by subtracting corporate taxes and mandatory principal debt repayments from operating income. FCF is the actual capital available for distribution to owners or reinvestment.

Prime Costs (%)

The sum of Cost of Goods Sold (food, beverage, packaging) and Total Labor (salaries, hourly wages, payroll taxes, and benefits). A universally recognized benchmark dictates that Prime Costs should remain below 60% of gross revenue to ensure sufficient margin is left to cover fixed overhead.

Capital Efficiency (Payback & ROI)

Payback Period identifies the exact decimal year in which cumulative Free Cash Flow eclipses the initial equity invested. Cash-on-Cash Return expresses the annual FCF as a percentage of that initial equity. If the project utilizes $0 in debt, these metrics accurately reflect the return profile of a 100% equity-funded venture.

Inflation & Compounding Drivers

The model projects asynchronous growth. Revenue and labor scale independently based on user inputs, while fixed operating expenses (Rent, Utilities, Insurance) compound automatically at the specified Fixed OPEX Inflation rate. This accurately stress-tests margin compression over a 5-year horizon.
